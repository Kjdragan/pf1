- **Definition**: Due process is a foundational constitutional doctrine ensuring fair legal proceedings and protection against arbitrary deprivation of life, liberty, or property.

---

- **Notice**: Individuals must receive informed notification of legal actions against them and the potential ramifications.
- **Hearing**: A fair and impartial hearing must be conducted, providing individuals the opportunity to present their case.
- **Legal Representation**: The right to legal counsel during proceedings is fundamental.

---

**Procedural Due Process**

- **Objective**: Guarantees adherence to fair procedures in legal actions.
- **Components**: Encompasses timely and adequate notice, an opportunity to be heard, and an impartial tribunal.
- **Examples**: Court trials, administrative hearings.

---

**Substantive Due Process**

- **Objective**: Shields fundamental rights from governmental encroachment.
- **Scope**: Encompasses rights not explicitly enumerated in the Constitution but regarded as essential.
- **Examples**: Privacy rights, marriage rights.
